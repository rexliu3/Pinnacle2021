Safety Articles
We have all heard of vehicle and driver safety, and the importance of remembering safety rules in our day-to-day job duties. But what about pedestrian safety?
Pedestrians encounter danger any time they walk on or near a roadway. Protect yourself and stay alert around vehicles.
At some point in the day, everyone is a pedestrian, and unfortunately pedestrian fatalities remain high
Safety is a key priority at UCLA. UCLA Transportation coordinates efforts with the UCLA Police Department to provide programs, materials and services focused on safety. This page provides information and links relating to those programs.
Walking is a healthy activity, but you need to know the rules of thumb of pedestrian safety.
While colleges and universities take active measures to protect students and maintain a safe campus, crime still happens.
According to the National Center for Education Statistics," the number of on-campus crimes per 10,000 full-time-equivalent (FTE) students [also] decreased, from 18.4 in 2013 to 17.9 in 2014.”
For more information, please call (310) 825-9800. Contact the UCLA Police Department to report a crime or other incident on campus, or to receive immediate help.
University of California - Los Angeles reported 827 safety-related incidents involving students on or near campus or other UCLA affiliated properties in 2019.
Students are far more likely to encounter property crime than they are to be the victims of violent crime over the course of an enrollment, our study found.
